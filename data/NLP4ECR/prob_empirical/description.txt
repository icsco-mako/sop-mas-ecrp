Sea-land coordinated empty container repositioning problem. From the perspective of a container shipping company, consider the repositioning of empty containers in a two-tier network consisting of seaports and dry ports. Formulate a multi-period network flow optimization model to minimize the company's total operating cost, including repositioning cost, holding cost, and container leasing cost.

Network topology: 3 seaports (Dalian Port, Yingkou Port, Dandong Port) and 10 dry ports (Shenyang, Anshan, Harbin, Changchun, Tonghua, Jilin, Tongliao, Chifeng, Daqing, Qiqihar), for a total of 13 nodes over 5 planning periods.

Empty container supply is generated from laden-container conversion: laden containers arriving at a node in the previous period become the empty container supply of that node in the current period. Laden-container departures from each node constitute its empty container demand.

Each node has a maximum storage capacity and incurs a per-period inventory holding cost. When a node faces an empty-container shortage, demand can be satisfied either by repositioning containers from other nodes or by leasing containers from a third party.

Empty-container repositioning follows specific time rules: repositioning between seaports requires a transit time of $\tau_{ij}$ periods (for example, Dalian Port to Dandong Port takes 2 periods); repositioning between a seaport and its hinterland dry ports, as well as repositioning between dry ports within the same hinterland, is completed within the current period.

Within each period, events occur in the following order: inherit inventory from the previous period, process the arrival of in-transit repositioning flows, convert laden-container arrivals into empty-container supply, and then make repositioning and leasing decisions.

Objective: minimize total operating cost = repositioning cost + holding cost + leasing cost.

Constraints include inventory flow balance, inventory capacity limits, and non-negativity of repositioning decisions.
